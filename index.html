Digital edition of Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley's Frankenstein

Part of the "Text as data" course at UAntwerp | 2025-2026


By Eva Andersen




Welcome to the mini digital edition of Mary Shelley's Frankenstein. It is an assignment of the course "Text as Data", taught at the University of Antwerp in the MA Digital Text Analysis. The Frankenstein book was first published in 1818 and would become a classic, going through many editions. On this website you find a selection of pages from the original manuscript which have been made into a digital edition by using TEI. The endocing manual used is the BDMP Manual. This digital edition is based on the edition found at the Shelley Godwin Archive. The original manuscript can be found at the Bodleian Library.

About this Frankenstein draft

Draft of Frankenstein, [August or September] – [?December] 1816. | Bodleian MS. Abinger c.56 (formerly designated Dep. 477/1) consists of 62 leaves originally bound in Notebook A, one of two now disbound notebooks in which Mary W. Shelley (MWS) drafted the two-volume version of Frankenstein. The draft in c.56 runs from the beginning to the end of Volume I, concluding just as the Creature is about to begin his tale to Victor.


Who are Mary Shelley and Percy Shelley?

Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley was an English novelist, most known for her book Frankenstein. You can read everything about her life and work on her Wikipedia page.

Percy Bysshe Shelley was an English writer and poet. He was married to Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley. You can read everything about his life and work on his wikipedia page.
